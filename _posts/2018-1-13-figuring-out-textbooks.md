As an early student I had the cliche dislike of textbooks and rightly so. 
They are often long, and stressful especially if there was going to be a test on it. 
However I still attempted to study them with little success. 
Sometimes highlighting the hell out of them, sometimes putting pieces of candy every few paragraphs to motivate me.

But as you would expect, I never learned anything and ended up wasting time. 
I realized that the students who do well actually take notes outside of the textbook 
(you would think this would have been obvious for me).

So over my Christmas break, and into the Spring semester I have focused on reading ahead in 
Planning Algorithms by Steven M. LaValle, and Linear Algebra and Its Applications 5th edition in preparation for my 
Grad Motion Planning course. I learned quite a lot about myself. 
I have noticed that I remember stuff many pages back in a chapter, and have much more detailed questions. 
During class I could pick out the students who read the textbook, and I could tell which chapters they read based on 
their questions. This made be feel more empowered knowing that I might be able to stay with the pace of the instructor.

![Intro to AI]({{ site.url | absolute_path}}/assets/images/2018-1-13-figuring-out-textbooks-img1.png)

I am now able to be more vocal about my confusion with the material, but I also end up answering my own questions, 
such as the words written in blue. One of the more major "wake up" calls was reading my linear algebra textbook. 
Since motion planning involves large amounts of linear algebra, 
and since I took the linear algebra class a full year ago, I decided to actually read the book. 
Back when I took linear algebra I relied on the instructor's lecture notes for study. 
You can guess that I did not perform well, and I avoided the textbook simply because 
I was too scared to spend hours on something I could just learning in class. This was a very wrong mentality. 
While reading the book, I have had more detailed questions and have actually ended up understanding the material 
better. So far each chapter only takes me 2-3 hours to read. I think that this is because I am less fearful that 
I might forget something for a test, or that I have gotten better at filtering textbook information for the parts that
I need in order to understand the material.
